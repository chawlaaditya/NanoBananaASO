# Visual Recipe

Use this as the default portrait recipe when the app needs a strong App Store screenshot starting point and there is no better app-specific art direction.

This recipe is derived from the strong `campaign-v3` set that produced good-looking, properly sized screenshots.

## Overall look

- dark editorial background
- strong white display type
- short support line directly beneath the headline
- one dominant phone or focal object
- one or two supporting UI fragments at most
- muted accent color, not neon
- consistent bottom chips or value tags

## Proven portrait structure

For a `1320x2868` export, use this as the baseline:

- headline block in the top 18 to 24 percent of the canvas
- support line directly below the headline
- dominant phone in the middle to lower-middle 50 to 65 percent of the canvas
- supporting UI fragment anchored beside or slightly overlapping the phone
- chips or compact claims along the lower band, above the safe bottom margin

## Safe placement guidance

For `1320x2868`:
- left and right safe margin: about `106px` minimum
- top safe margin: about `172px` minimum
- bottom safe margin: about `230px` minimum

Keep all must-read text, chips, and focal visuals inside these bounds.

## What made the set work

- headlines were large, bold, and extremely scannable
- support lines stayed short
- the phone was always visually dominant
- each slide used only a small amount of supporting UI outside the phone
- adjacent slides changed the phone placement or support-fragment placement
- the visual family stayed consistent through type, chips, mood, and spacing
- the normalized exports still looked correct because the source compositions were crop-safe

## Default slide rhythm

Use this variety pattern unless the app needs something more specific:

- slide 1: centered hero phone
- slide 2: phone offset left or right with stacked fragment cards opposite
- slide 3: asymmetric phone with one larger supporting fragment
- slide 4: darker immersive slide with one oversized metric or feature fragment
- slide 5: split composition with phone on one side and proof/progress fragments opposite
- slide 6: recap or insight slide with more centered product focus
- slide 7: typography-led value slide with no fake app screen

## Common ways to ruin this pattern

- too much text
- chips repeating the support line
- too many floating fragments
- backgrounds that become the main event
- tiny phone scale
- text too close to the edges
- relying on normalization to fix a bad source layout
